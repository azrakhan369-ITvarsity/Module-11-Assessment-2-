BRAZIL

    Location
  • Brazil is located in South America, covering nearly half of the continent. It is bordered by every other South American country except Chile and Ecuador, with the Atlantic Ocean to its east.

    • History
  • Brazil's history began with indigenous tribes who lived there for thousands of years before the Portuguese arrived in 1500. The Portuguese claimed the land, and Brazil became a colony, with its economy initially driven by sugarcane plantations and later by gold and coffee. Enslaved Africans were brought in large numbers, shaping Brazilian culture and demographics. In 1822, Brazil declared independence from Portugal, forming the Empire of Brazil. The country transitioned to a republic in 1889, ending the monarchy and abolishing slavery the previous year. Throughout the 20th century, Brazil underwent periods of political upheaval, including military rule from 1964 to 1985, before returning to democracy. Today, Brazil is known for its cultural diversity, vast natural resources, and influence as the largest country in South America.

    • Tourist Attraction
  • The number one tourist attraction in Brazil is Christ the Redeemer (Cristo Redentor), a massive statue of Jesus Christ overlooking Rio de Janeiro. Standing 98 feet tall on Mount Corcovado, this iconic statue is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World. It offers breathtaking views of Rio and its stunning beaches, mountains, and rainforests, making it a must-visit destination for tourists.

    • Fun Fact
  • A fun fact about Brazil is that it is home to the Amazon Rainforest, the largest tropical rainforest in the world. The Amazon, often called the "lungs of the Earth," produces about 20% of the world’s oxygen and is home to an incredibly diverse range of wildlife and plant species, many of which are found nowhere else on Earth.

Brazils Flavours

Brazilian cuisine is a melting pot of indigenous, Portuguese, African, and immigrant influences, known for its rich flavors and regional diversity. Feijoada, a hearty black bean stew with pork, is Brazil’s national dish, typically served with rice, collard greens, and orange slices. Other popular dishes include pão de queijo (cheese bread), acarajé (black-eyed pea fritters stuffed with shrimp), and moqueca (a seafood stew). In the Amazon region, exotic ingredients like açaí berries, cassava, and tropical fruits add unique flavors to traditional dishes. Brazilian cuisine is celebrated for its bold, comforting flavors and the use of fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

The Must Try Dish I Discovered in Brazil

FEJÁO TROPEIRO

Feijoada or feijoada completa is Brazil's national dish, a hearty stew featuring lamb and black beans. The dish is consumed throughout the country, and every family in Brazil has their own, special recipe. Sautéed greens, cheese rolls, rice, and fresh, sliced oranges are served as an accompaniment to the smoked lamb and richly flavoured black beans.
